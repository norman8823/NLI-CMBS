## Executive Summary

BMARK 2024-V6 is a $729.8M conduit transaction collateralized by 129 first-lien commercial mortgage loans across Self Storage, Retail, Office, and Mixed Use property types. The pool is performing with 0.0% delinquency and no specially serviced loans as of the March 2026 reporting period. The WA coupon stands at 6.1% with a WA DSCR of 1.45x, though 29.5% interest-only exposure and significant maturity concentration in 2035-2036 warrant continued monitoring.

## Deal Performance Overview

Current UPB totals $729.8M against an original UPB of $729.8M, reflecting a newly originated pool with minimal paydown. The WA coupon is 6.1% and WA DSCR is 1.45x, indicating adequate debt service coverage across the collateral. WA LTV data is not yet available for this reporting period. WA occupancy is 94.2%.

Interest-only loans represent 29.5% of the pool (38 loans), concentrated among the larger balance assets including four of the top five loans. Balloon loans comprise 48.1% of the pool (62 loans). The average loan size is $5.7M, with a range from under $1.0M for smaller Self Storage assets to $70.0M for the largest Office loan.

Property type distribution skews toward Retail at 30.3% of UPB ($221.1M, 24 loans), Self Storage at 24.3% ($177.5M, 54 loans), and Office at 21.2% ($154.5M, 5 loans). The remaining collateral includes Mixed Use at 8.5% ($62.0M), Manufactured Housing at 0.8% ($6.0M), Lodging at 1.8% ($13.5M), and Multifamily at 2.5% ($18.5M). The 25 loans classified as CH (Co-op Housing) contribute 10.5% of UPB at $76.7M.

The five Office loans, while representing only 5 of 129 loans, account for $154.5M or 21.2% of pool UPB. Three of these are located in Manhattan (215 Park Avenue South, 525 7th Avenue, and 50 West 23rd Street), contributing geographic and single-asset concentration risk to the pool.

## Delinquency & Special Servicing

The pool reports zero delinquencies across all buckets (30-day, 60-day, 90+ day) as of March 2026. No loans are specially serviced. No loans are in foreclosure or REO.

The clean delinquency profile is expected given that all loans in the pool were originated in late 2025 and early 2026. Seasoning remains minimal at less than six months for the majority of collateral. Credit performance should be monitored as loans move through their first 12-24 months, when initial lease rollover and borrower-level operating challenges typically emerge. The 54 Self Storage loans and 25 Co-op Housing loans are granular and individually small, reducing concentration risk from any single delinquency event.

## Maturity & Refinancing Risk

The maturity schedule is concentrated in two primary vintage years:

- **2035**: 23 loans totaling $316.1M (43.3% of pool UPB)
- **2036**: 36 loans totaling $361.3M (49.5% of pool UPB)
- **2029**: 1 loan totaling $7.5M (1.0% of pool UPB)
- **2050**: 2 loans totaling $44.9M (6.2% of pool UPB)

Approximately 92.8% of pool UPB matures between October 2035 and March 2036, creating a significant maturity wall. Refinancing conditions at that time will be the primary determinant of pool credit performance. The two 2050 maturities — including the $38.9M U-Haul Portfolio — carry 25-year amortization schedules, partially mitigating their extended term risk.

No loans mature within the next 24 months.

## Outlook

BMARK 2024-V6 enters surveillance with a clean credit profile — zero delinquencies, adequate WA DSCR at 1.45x, and stable occupancy at 94.2%. The transaction benefits from granular collateral (129 loans, $5.7M average) and property type diversification across Self Storage, Retail, Office, and Mixed Use.

Key items to monitor going forward: (1) the concentrated 2035-2036 maturity wall ($677.4M, 92.8% of UPB), which will require favorable CRE capital markets for orderly refinancing; (2) single-asset Office exposure in Manhattan ($135.0M across three properties), given ongoing structural headwinds in the office sector; and (3) the elevated IO share among the largest loans, which limits near-term principal paydown. Performance through the first four quarterly reporting periods will establish the baseline credit trajectory for this pool.
